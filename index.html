How to Use the Position Size Calculator

Enter your account balance, your maximum risk per trade (typically 1–2%), your entry price, and your stop loss price. The calculator instantly shows you how many shares to buy so you never risk more than your defined limit.

What Is Position Sizing?

Position sizing is the process of determining how many shares, lots, or contracts to buy for a given trade based on your account size and risk tolerance. Proper position sizing is the foundation of professional risk management and helps traders survive losing streaks without blowing up their account.

Position Size Formula

The formula used by this calculator:

Position Size = ( Account Balance × Risk % ) ÷ ( Entry Price − Stop Loss Price )

Frequently Asked Questions

What percentage should I risk per trade?

Most professional traders risk between 1% and 2% of their account per trade. This allows you to withstand a long losing streak without significantly depleting your capital.

Does this work for forex and crypto?

Yes. While the result is shown in "shares", the same math applies to any market. For forex, the output represents units; for crypto, it represents coins or tokens.

Why does position size decrease when I widen my stop loss?

A wider stop loss means each share carries more risk. To keep your total dollar risk the same, you must buy fewer shares. This is why tight, well-placed stop losses allow for larger position sizes.
